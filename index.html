Who We Are

About YCM

Empowering young people through creativity, opportunity and development.

Our Mission

Pathways to creativity, confidence & opportunity

YCM is a youth-led organisation dedicated to helping young people discover their potential through creativity, personal development, and positive community engagement.

We provide accessible opportunities for young people to explore music, art, writing, performance, fitness, and creative expression in supportive environments that encourage growth, confidence, and self-belief.

Our mission is to create pathways that help young people develop practical skills, express themselves authentically, and build brighter futures. Through workshops, mentorship, creative projects, community events, and our long-term vision of a mobile creative studio, we aim to bring opportunities directly to the communities that need them most.

At YCM, we believe creativity is more than a hobby — it is a tool for personal development, connection, wellbeing, and opportunity.

Who's Behind YCM

The people running the movement

Aj

Founder

I started YCM for the younger version of myself. Growing up, I didn't always make choices I'm proud of, and I've seen first-hand — through family who've been caught up in knife crime, both as people who've made mistakes and as victims of it — how easily a young person's path can be shaped by their environment rather than their potential.

Art saved a lot of that for me. It gave me somewhere to put what I was feeling instead of acting on it, and a reason to believe in something beyond where I was from. That's what I want YCM to be for other young people: a space where creativity becomes a way out, a way up, and a way to be heard.

Tobi

Editing & Content Course Lead

I run our editing course, where I teach young people the practical side of content creation — from editing technique to finding real clients and building a portfolio that actually gets work.

We cover the resources and tools people need to start editing properly, along with how to optimise content for art and creative work specifically. Editing is a genuinely profitable, in-demand skill, and it's also its own form of creation — I want young people to see it that way, not just as a technical chore.

Keeping Young People Safe

Our Safeguarding Commitment

YCM works directly with young people, so safeguarding isn't an afterthought — it underpins everything we run.

Every young person who takes part in a YCM session, workshop, or event deserves to feel safe, respected, and supported.

We are committed to creating environments — whether in the studio, the gym, or out in the community — where young people are protected from harm and where any concerns are taken seriously and acted on promptly.

YCM operates a clear safeguarding policy that applies to all staff, mentors, volunteers, and anyone who comes into contact with young people through our programmes. This policy is reviewed regularly and applies equally across all of our activities, whether in-person or online.

What this means in practice:

All staff, mentors, and volunteers working directly with young people undergo appropriate background checks (DBS, or your region's equivalent) before taking part in sessions.

We maintain a named Designated Safeguarding Lead who is the first point of contact for any concerns raised by a young person, parent, or member of the public.

Sessions involving under-18s follow appropriate ratios of staff to young people, and parents/guardians are kept informed of programme content and any changes.

Reporting A Concern

If something doesn't feel right

If you ever have a safeguarding concern about YCM — as a parent, participant, or member of the public — please contact us directly and confidentially.

Safeguarding Contact Email: youngcreatorsltd@gmail.com
We aim to acknowledge all safeguarding concerns promptly and treat them with the seriousness they deserve.

How We Fund The Movement

Sales, fundraising & earning through creativity

At YCM we don't just teach skills — we teach young people how to turn those skills into income, while making sure the movement sustains itself.

How We Fundraise

Community-first, transparent fundraising

Everything we raise goes back into the programmes, the people who run them, and the long-term goal of the Mobile Creative Studio.

YCM fundraises directly within the communities we serve — through events, showcases, street fundraising with Square, and contribution drives like the one on this site.

We are transparent about where money goes. Every pound raised is directed toward session costs, equipment, paying our course leads fairly, and building toward the studio van that will bring opportunities to young people who can't always come to us.

We don't rely on a single income stream. Contributions from the public, partnerships with local organisations, and revenue from our own programmes all combine to keep YCM running and growing.

What funds are used for:

Course leads and mentors are paid fairly for their time. We believe that if we're teaching young people that their skills have value, we have to demonstrate that ourselves — which means the people delivering sessions are compensated properly.

Equipment, venue hire, and resources are covered so that young people never have to pay out of pocket to access what we offer. Accessibility is non-negotiable at YCM.

A portion of every fundraising round is ring-fenced for the Mobile Creative Studio fund — our long-term goal of bringing the studio directly to communities.
